Circular EconomyMaterial Science

In-Line Sprue Regrinding & Circular Integration

VALEO · February 2026
Overview

This project aimed to close the material loop on the injection molding line by reintegrating sprue and runner waste directly back into production. The challenge was to do this without compromising part quality — requiring rigorous validation of regrind-to-virgin material ratios.

Project Timeline
Phase 1 — Audit & Press Selection
Mapped material consumption across presses. Selected optimal machines based on throughput, material type, and workshop logistics to minimize transport waste.
Phase 2 — Ratio Testing
Tested multiple regrind-to-virgin ratios on trial runs. Measured melt flow, shrinkage, and surface quality at each ratio level.
Phase 3 — Mechanical Validation
Validated tensile strength, impact resistance, and dimensional tolerances against OEM specifications for each qualified ratio.
Phase 4 — Production Qualification
Formally qualified the process and documented parameters for standard operating procedures. Approved for full production rollout.
Key Benefits
  • ✓
    Reduced virgin material consumption and raw material cost per part
  • ✓
    Eliminated sprue/runner waste — direct circular loop on the production floor
  • ✓
    No measurable quality degradation at validated regrind ratios
  • ✓
    Created a repeatable qualification framework reusable for other materials

Injection Cycle Time & Process Optimization

VALEO · December 2025
Overview

A systematic analysis of the full injection cycle to identify and eliminate idle time without any capital investment. The project combined mold kinematics review with injection phase parameter optimization to maximize machine utilization.

Project Timeline
Phase 1 — Cycle Mapping
Recorded and broke down each cycle phase: injection, packing, cooling, mold open/close, ejection, and robot extraction. Identified idle time pockets.
Phase 2 — Mold Kinematics Review
Analyzed mold open/close speed profiles and ejector sequences. Refined movement curves to reduce dead time while maintaining part integrity.
Phase 3 — Parameter Tuning
Adjusted injection speed, packing pressure profile, and cooling time based on thermal simulations and in-process measurements.
Phase 4 — Validation & Measurement
Ran production trials, measured cycle time and part quality. Average idle time reduced by up to 15%.
Key Benefits
  • ✓
    Up to 15% reduction in average idle time per cycle
  • ✓
    Increased machine throughput with zero capital expenditure
  • ✓
    No change to part quality or dimensional tolerances
  • ✓
    Parameters documented as new production standard

Lean / SMEDMuda Elimination

Lean Manufacturing: Muda Hunting & SMED Implementation

VALEO · October 2025
Overview

A full lean manufacturing initiative targeting two types of waste: operational Muda in day-to-day tasks, and excessive changeover time on 400T and 100T presses. The project combined operator workflow auditing with physical hardware upgrades to achieve rapid, measurable results.

Project Timeline
Phase 1 — Muda Audit
Observed and filmed operator movements during production and changeover. Classified waste types (waiting, transport, motion, over-processing) and ranked by frequency and impact.
Phase 2 — SMED Analysis
Separated internal and external changeover tasks. Converted as many internal tasks to external as possible to reduce machine downtime during mold swaps.
Phase 3 — Hardware Implementation
Installed magnetic and hydraulic quick-clamping systems, multi-coupling water manifolds for simultaneous cooling connection, and EOAT quick-change plates on the robot arm.
Phase 4 — Measurement & Documentation
Timed before/after changeover durations. Up to 30 minutes saved per changeover. New procedures standardized across the workshop.
Key Benefits
  • ✓
    Up to 30 minutes saved per press changeover — documented and verified
  • ✓
    Significant OEE improvement through reduced planned downtime
  • ✓
    Operator ergonomics improved by eliminating unnecessary motion waste
  • ✓
    EOAT quick-change reduced robot reconfiguration to under 2 minutes
  • ✓
    New SOPs written and deployed across the production team

KPI Performance Tracking System

VALEO · September 2025
Overview

Designed and deployed a custom real-time KPI tracking system to give the production team live visibility into machine and process performance. The tool tracks downtime causes, changeover durations, and micro-stop frequency to enable fast, data-driven decisions.

Project Timeline
Phase 1 — KPI Definition
Worked with production supervisors to define the most impactful KPIs: OEE components (availability, performance, quality), changeover time, and micro-stop frequency by cause code.
Phase 2 — Data Architecture
Designed the data collection structure and input workflows. Defined how operators log events in real time at the machine level.
Phase 3 — Dashboard Build
Built the tracking interface and visualization layer. Output includes live production boards, shift summaries, and trend analysis over rolling periods.
Phase 4 — Deployment & Training
Rolled out to the production floor. Trained operators and team leaders on data entry and dashboard interpretation.
Key Benefits
  • ✓
    Real-time production visibility for operators and supervisors
  • ✓
    Faster root-cause identification on downtime events
  • ✓
    Trend data enabling proactive maintenance scheduling
  • ✓
    Standardized performance reporting across all tracked machines

Material Characterization of Recycled Plastics for Injection Molding

Final Year Project · June 2025
Overview

An academic-industrial research project investigating whether recycled plastic grades could replace virgin materials in injection-molded automotive components without sacrificing quality. The project produced a practical qualification methodology applicable in real production environments.

Project Timeline
Phase 1 — Literature & Material Selection
Reviewed existing research on recycled polymer behavior. Selected representative grades of post-industrial recycled PP and PA for testing.
Phase 2 — Mechanical Characterization
Performed tensile, flexural, and impact testing on recycled vs virgin specimens. Documented degradation patterns as a function of recycling cycles.
Phase 3 — Process Compatibility Testing
Injected test parts using recycled grades. Analyzed shrinkage, warpage, surface quality, and dimensional accuracy vs virgin benchmarks.
Phase 4 — Qualification Framework
Defined acceptance criteria and a decision tree for qualifying recycled materials in production. Validated against VALEO internal quality standards.
Key Benefits
  • ✓
    Established which recycled grades are viable for automotive-grade injection molding
  • ✓
    Created a reusable qualification methodology applicable beyond this study
  • ✓
    Provided a quantified basis for sustainability decisions in material sourcing
  • ✓
    Bridged academic materials science with real industrial process constraints
